  • Overview

    • What does our model aim to accomplish?

    And what questions can it answer?

    Research Questions


    We primarily aim to answer...

  • How do the Philippines' CO2 emissions from cement and fossil fuels correlate with its GDP over time, and are these emission trends aligned with the rates of economic growth from 1946 to 2022?


    • And with that information...

  • Does the Philippines emit CO2 disproportionately compared to its GDP and GDP growth, relative to other and similar economies?

    • Problem and Response

    As a country with a rapidly developing economy, a steadily growing population, and a unique vulnerability to climate change, the Philippines now, more than ever, needs to be wary of how its growth in industry affects our carbon emissions.

    So C0DEBABE5 has created (is creating?) a model that takes the Philippines' and other similar and relevant countries' economic and emission-related data and gauges their relationship in order to predict what future carbon emission data might look like if no action is taken.

    Intention

    Utilizing economic indicators and historical emissions data, our model aims to forecast future CO2 emissions in the Philippines. This predictive capability will allow us to benchmark our emissions against similar economies, assessing whether our outputs are proportionate to our economic growth. Additionally, the model will evaluate the effectiveness of recent environmental policies by comparing actual emissions to the predicted values. This analysis will help determine if these policies have successfully mitigated emissions as intended.

  • The Data and its Collection

    • Where did our data come from?

    And what features of such data were the team concerned with?

    The World Bank Dataset

    The World Bank Data website is a platform provided by the World Bank Group that offers free access to a wide range of data on global development. The team had acquired several necessary development indicators from this data source, particularly economy-related data for correlation purposes.

    OWID CO2 Emissions Dataset

    This dataset contains information on global carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions over time across various countries. Such data consists of metrics obtained from Our World in Data and its organization. Data of interest consists of indicators concerning Greenhouse gas emissions to be correlated to other data features.

    The team has collected necessary data from both datasets, of which are to be correlated and modeled to achieve the project's objectives.

    Features of Interest

    Dataset Size

    The combined dataset created from the two data sources resulted into a size of 1111 data points. Note that this size was the result after data filtering and pre-processing

    Time Period

    The data obtained are filtered between the years 1946-2022 in adherence to data recency and availability.

    Selected Countries

    The countries included in this project consist of: Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, Cambodia, Mexico, Brazil, India, United States, Kenya, South Africa, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, China. The choice of such nations is done considering data availability and inclusion of various economic classifications, growth, and productions.

    Gross Domestic Product

    The Gross Domestic Product or GDP is an indicator used to measure the general economic performance of a country, making this feature to be highly relevant in the aims of the project.

    Following the dataset in OWID and The World Bank, the GDP is measured using the US Dollar currency.

    Fossil Fuel CO2 Emissions

    This feature consists of the sum of CO2 emissions from gas, coal, and oil sources. Energy production, industrial processes, and transportation are some examples of sectors of which fossil fuel combustion occurs of which are necessary for the economy to run and grow.

    Such data obtained from the OWID dataset are measured in million tonnes.

    Cement CO2 Emissions

    This feature consists of CO2 emissions from the production of cement. Apart from the previous sources of CO2 emissions, cement production is a great contributing factor to generation of Greenhouse gasses, whereas about 8% of the total CO2 emissions are due to cement productions as reported by the CBS News last January 2023.

    Also, similar to the previous feature, cement CO2 emissions are measured in million tonnes.

    Data Preprocessing

    After obtaining the necessary datasets, the data was filtered and combined. As previously stated, data ranging from 1946-2022 were used given the selected countries. The OWID dataset was highly prioritized for data collection, while The World Bank dataset was generally used to fill out missing data observed from the former.

    Enumerating the steps done in the combining stage:

    1. Gas, oil and coal CO2 emissions were summed, producing fossil fuel column in the combined dataset. Note that cement CO2 emissions were kept separate but is still included in the combined dataset as a separate column.
    2. GDP was primarily taken from the OWID dataset, if available
    3. If GDP from OWID is unavailable, The World Bank dataset is consulted. Note that data retrieved from the later is converted into the US dollar value of the year 2011.
    4. If data is not available to both datasets, the specific row or record is excluded.
